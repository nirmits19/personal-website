Your First Essay

Begin here. Write what you actually think — not what sounds correct, but what survives honest scrutiny. The essay is a form of thinking aloud.

Every great essay starts with a question the writer cannot yet answer. The act of writing is the act of finding out. Don't wait until you have something to say — write to discover what it is.

01 / 04 Currently

Meditations

Marcus Aurelius

Written for no one but himself, yet it outlasted the empire he commanded. Each entry a small battle against distraction and vanity.

Book V is where it becomes human — the reluctance to rise in the morning, the frustration with others, the self-recrimination. The emperor, struggling like the rest of us.

02 / 04 To Read

Inferno

Dante Alighieri

A descent through Hell as a journey toward understanding. The damned are not punished arbitrarily — each soul inhabits the logical conclusion of the life it chose.

Midway upon the journey of our life, I found myself within a forest dark, for the straightforward pathway had been lost. It begins with disorientation — and never quite resolves it.

03 / 04 Read

The Enchiridion

Epictetus

A short handbook for living. Its brevity is the point.

Some things are in our control; others are not. This is the whole of it. The difficulty is remembering which is which in the moment it matters.
