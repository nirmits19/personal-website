I. Of Myself

Here I set down, for those who may find it of use, some account of who I am — not to boast, for what is there to boast of in the brief candle of one life, but so that you may know the hand behind these words.

I am a builder of things, a seeker of ideas, and a student of whatever the world will consent to teach me. I believe that the examined life is worth living, and that good work done quietly is its own reward.

"Waste no more time arguing about what a good man should be. Be one." — Marcus Aurelius, Meditations

III. Interests

The things that occupy my mind when the work is done — and sometimes while it is not.

⚡

Technology

Software, systems, and the art of building tools that endure. The machine is a canvas; the engineer, a sculptor of logic.

🏛️

Philosophy

Stoicism, epistemology, and the ancient question of how one ought to live. The examined life remains the only kind worth having.

📐

Mathematics

The language in which, as Galileo observed, the great book of nature is written. Elegant in its certainty; humbling in its depth.
